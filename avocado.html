Avocado Toast or a 3 Bedroom House?
The Greatest Debate of 2024 (so far)

Ever wondered how a humble green fruit became the reigning champion of brunches, the muse of countless Instagram feeds, and the subject of heated debates over guacamole recipes? Or questioned whether the deposit on your 2 bedroom basement flat could've been spent a little differently? Ok, maybe you haven't - but I know you are now.

_____________

Lets start simple. Its useful to gauge an understanding on the changes in house prices across the UK before we can begin to relate this to our beloved green friends. Using ONS Data we can easily tell that there has been a steady increase in house prices. It's clear to see that as time progresses, the house price range widens and diverges from the regression line, indicating an increasing variability or dispersion of the data points over time. We call this heteroscedasticity. As time goes on, the variability of the data points (residuals) around the regression line increases, indicating heteroscedasticity. In 1968, the data is clustered, which is the complete opposite in 2021. By hovering over the data points, we can see which regions the data aligns to.

An R2 value of of 0.66 means that approximately 66% of the variation in average house prices can be explained by the date variable in the regression model. In other words, this suggests that about 66% of the fluctuations or changes observed in average house prices over time can be attributed to the chronological progression of dates in the dataset.
