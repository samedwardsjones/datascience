Avocado Toast or a 3 Bedroom House?
The Greatest Debate of 2024 (so far)

Ever wondered how a humble green fruit became the reigning champion of brunches, the muse of countless Instagram feeds, and the subject of heated debates over guacamole recipes? Or questioned whether the deposit on your 2 bedroom basement flat could've been spent a little differently? Ok, maybe you haven't, but I know you're now thinking about it.

Lets start simple. Its useful to gauge an understanding on the changes in house prices across the UK before we can begin to relate this to our beloved green friends. Using ONS Data we can easily tell that there has been a steady increase in house prices. It's clear to see that as time progresses, the house price range widens and diverges from the regression line, indicating an increasing variability or dispersion of the data points over time. We call this heteroscedasticity. As time goes on, the variability of the data points (residuals) around the regression line increases, indicating heteroscedasticity. In 1968, the data is clustered, which is the complete opposite in 2021.By hovering over the data points, we can see which regions the data aligns to. More on this later...

An R2 value of of 0.66 means that approximately 66% of the variation in average house prices can be explained by the date variable in the regression model. In other words, this suggests that about 66% of the fluctuations or changes observed in average house prices over time can be attributed to the chronological progression of dates in the dataset.

Now, lets understand the realm of Avocados

The top 10 populated US cities are matched with data from the non-profit organisation the Gun Violence Archive (GVA), which compiles data from law enforcement, media, and government sources from all 50 states, plotting fatal and non-fatal shootings from 2014-2022.

Despite restrictive gun laws, Chicago accounts for 57% of all non-fatal shootings out of the top 10 most populated cities, and 9% of all cities and counties in the GVA dataset (2014-2022).

Mapping Gun Violence

Reported incidents of fatal and non-fatal shootings collected from the Chicago Data Portal are shown at neighbourhood levels on an interactive map. Austin, which is Chicago's largest neighbourhood, located far from the city centre, suffers from the most reported gun crimes out of all the neighbourhoods, accounting for 8% of all reported crimes in the 77 neighbourhoods between 2014-22.

Mapping income disparities

Austin has long suffered from severe disinvestment because of white flight, making it a hub of concentrated poverty, shown by its drastically different income distribution than to the City of Chicago. But can poverty be a predictor of gun violence? In the maps below, we can see that neighbourhoods with high gun crimes tend to have lower incomes, but the South-East of Chicago sees low median incomes with relatively low reported gun crimes.

Social mobility is also mapped, seeing if children who grew up with parents in the lowest 25th percentile of national income distribution can escape poverty. The results here fit the gun crime map more consistently. Climbing up the economic ladder is harder. The once since achievable American Dream is just a fantasy for many people in Chicago, and across America. Structural inequalities, often rooted in racism, mean marginalised individuals are more likely to live in hyper segregated poor neighbourhoods with limited economic opportunity, fuelling gun violence.

Other socioeconomic disparities

Socioeconomic disparities across Chicago are not limited to income. Educational attainment and children receiving food stamps is mapped below. Exposure to gun violence can detrimentally affect students' educational attainment, further reducing their economic opportunities, creating a cycle of violence for already suffering communities. High poverty concentration can have detrimental implications, increasing the risk of youth firearm deaths, and as tracts in Austin have over 50% of households with children under 18 receiving government Food Stamps, the neighbourhood is especially vulnerable to youth related gun violence.

Data Challenges

Due to the GVA's database query method, only 2000 rows of data could be collected at a time. A data analysist hand collected and cleaned comprehensive data, and I collected and hand cleaned the rest. Data cleaning overcame the issue of city names not being the same in both data sets and aggregated daily statistics into annual data.

I initially used API keys for the US census bureau data to plot poverty rates. However, the household median income dataset was easily accessible to be downloaded from the advanced database.

As lower upward mobility is linked to gun violence, the Opportunity Insights estimations of income by parental income is very useful. Census tracts in the North-East of Chicago have missing data for social mobility, which is a limitation. However, as this contains the richest neighbourhoods, with high educational attainment, and relatively low levels of gun violence, it is not the target of this research.

Conclusions

Patterns between high levels of gun violence and low social mobility can be seen geographically in Chicago. However, causality may not necessarily be taken, as socioeconomic measures and gun violence may be two-way causal. Nonetheless, the research here provides beneficial insight into the impacts and causes of gun violence, a pressing issue which deserves further exploration.
